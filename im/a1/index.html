Tetrageddon


tetrageddon.com
  1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

    The first thing that caught my eye once opening the website is a loading screen which says "It's now safe to turn off your computer." This sets up the premise of the website being a play on older websites from the late 90's and early 2000's. It creates a overall both nostalgic yet comedic tone which is continued in the main website with the countless pop ups, old style text boxes and grey/blue colour palate of the boarders.

  2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

    Clicking buttons to close pop-ups.
    Being redirected to otherwebsites with mini experiences such; screaming into the void, and reading an unagreeable User License Agreement.
    Clicking cookies.
    Clicking the 'Cybermonkey' which plays very loud muisc and monkey sound effects.

  3. What part of the experince did you spend the most time engaging with?

    The part if spent the most time interactive with as the pop-up windows which do not allow you to close them, but rather agree to whatever they are saying. The often redirect you somewhere else or lead to a comedic interaction on the main site.

  4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction wit the experience?

    Other than turning down my volume because the sound effects were too loud, the most common action with the website was again clicking and navigating the pop-ups and external sites which moved around the screen.

  5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experince?

    My impression of the intended primary goal is that the creator was intending to create a mock website in the style of late 90's/early 2000's websites about the end of the world, which pulls at nostalgic strings for people who used the internet back during that time.

  6. What is your impression of how the interactive experience communicates its primary goal?

    The experince communicates this through the very bold and dated design of the website graphics, from the background, boarders, font choice, pop-up windows and sound effects which are often too loud or distorted, simillar to how sound effects could be on older websites.

  7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interactive with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

    From my experience with the website, I believe it should be interacted with once but for around 15 minutes so that users have time to explore the website and see how many hidden jokes, redirects and pop-up windows they can discover. The site is intended to be enjoyed from a comedic point of view so using the site multiple times would loose its comedic value.

  8. What is your impression of how the interactive experience communicates how it should be interacted with over time?

    The website is very good at drawing users in with a very simple premise of clicking windows and exploring for hidden details. It's exciting to find a redirect to antoher site and the hiddle jokes such as the 'Cybermonkey' are interesting in the own merit.
